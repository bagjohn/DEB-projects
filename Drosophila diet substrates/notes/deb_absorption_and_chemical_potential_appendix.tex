This appendix merges and harmonizes material on chemical potential and tracer-based glucose absorption into a single DEB-consistent reference document. It is intended as a methods/appendix section for manuscripts, theses, or model documentation.

\section*{A1. Conceptual separation (DEB notation)}

We distinguish strictly between
\begin{itemize}
  \item the \emph{food density} $X$ (substrate density, typically in C-mol\,cm$^{-3}$),
  \item the \emph{chemical potential of food} $\mu_X$ (energy per C-mol of substrate; J\,C-mol$^{-1}$), and
  \item the \emph{environmental energy density} $\varepsilon_X$ (derived quantity)
  \begin{equation}
    \varepsilon_X \equiv \mu_X X, \qquad [\varepsilon_X] = \mathrm{J\,cm^{-3}}.
  \end{equation}
\end{itemize}

Only $X$ enters the DEB functional response; $\varepsilon_X$ is a convenient derived quantity when diets are specified by mass or molarity.

\section*{A2. Diet context for tracer experiments}

All $^{14}$C-glucose experiments in Kaun et al. (2007) were conducted on
\begin{itemize}
  \item yeast paste (2:1 water:yeast, w/w),
  \item with $^{14}$C-labelled glucose added as a tracer.
\end{itemize}

Thus the diet is mixed and nutritionally complete, glucose is not the sole nutrient, and the tracer probes carbohydrate-specific ingestion and retention rather than total feeding or total assimilation.

\section*{A3. Chemical potential used}

For carbohydrates (glucose / fructose), we adopt
\begin{equation}
  \mu_{\mathrm{carb}} = 4.68\times10^{5}\,\mathrm{J\,C\text{-}mol^{-1}}.
\end{equation}
This value is consistent with the energetic densities used elsewhere in the diet reconstruction and allows direct translation between tracer uptake (C-mol) and energy (J).

\section*{A5. Conversion to C-moles and energy}

We use
\begin{itemize}
  \item glucose: $n_C = 6$,
  \item $1\,\mathrm{fmol} = 10^{-15}\,\mathrm{mol}$.
\end{itemize}

For a value $B$ (fmol glucose per larva), the carbon amount and energy are
\begin{align}
  n_C &= 6 B\times10^{-15}\quad [\mathrm{C\text{-}mol}],\\
  E &= \mu_{\mathrm{carb}}\, n_C \quad [\mathrm{J}].
\end{align}

Using the digitized absorption values, we obtain

\section*{A6. Average fluxes (DEB-style interpretation}

Taking the 15\,min feeding window as
\begin{equation}
  \Delta t_{\mathrm{feed}} = 15\,\mathrm{min} = 0.0104167\,\mathrm{d},
\end{equation}
we define the average ingestion flux of glucose-derived carbon as
\begin{equation}
  \dot J_X^{(15)} = \frac{n_{C,\mathrm{intake}}}{\Delta t_{\mathrm{feed}}}\quad [\mathrm{C\text{-}mol\,d^{-1}}],
\end{equation}
while the average net absorbed flux is
\begin{equation}
  \dot J_{\mathrm{abs}}^{(15)} = \frac{n_{C,\mathrm{abs}}}{\Delta t_{\mathrm{feed}}}.
\end{equation}
The corresponding energy flux is
\begin{equation}
  \dot p = \mu_{\mathrm{carb}}\,\dot J.
\end{equation}

Numerically this yields

\begin{tabular}{lllll}
\toprule
Genotype & $\dot J_X^{(15)}$ (C-mol\,d$^{-1}$) & $\dot p_X^{(15)}$ (J\,d$^{-1}$) & $\dot J_{\mathrm{abs}}^{(15)}$ (C-mol\,d$^{-1}$) & $\dot p_{\mathrm{abs}}^{(15)}$ (J\,d$^{-1}$) \\
\midrule
for$^R$ & $3.46\times10^{-10}$ & $1.62\times10^{-4}$ & $1.78\times10^{-10}$ & $8.35\times10^{-5}$ \\

\section*{A7. Absorption efficiency proxy}

A robust, dimensionless quantity summarising the tracer assay is the absorption (retention) fraction
\begin{equation}
  \eta_{\mathrm{abs}} = \frac{\text{Absorption}}{\text{Intake}}.
\end{equation}
Using the digitized values gives
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Genotype & $\eta_{\mathrm{abs}}$ \\
\midrule
for$^R$ & 0.516 \\
for$^S$ & 0.161 \\
for$^{S2}$ & 0.174 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

This quantity captures post-ingestive carbohydrate retention efficiency and can be used in DEB-inspired models as a genotype-specific modifier of effective assimilation for carbohydrates, without reinterpreting it as total reserve assimilation.

\section*{A8. DEB-consistent placement}

In DEB terms, the main points are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Diet context: high food density (yeast paste) implies $f\approx 1$ for total food.
  \item The tracer informs pathway-specific processing (carbohydrate), not overall feeding capacity.
  \item The data constrain relative differences between genotypes, not absolute $\dot p_{Am}$ or $K$.
\end{itemize}

A DEB-consistent usage is to treat $\eta_{\mathrm{abs}}$ as a proxy for a carbohydrate-specific effective assimilation factor, while keeping total assimilation capacity and reserve dynamics anchored by other lines of evidence.
