# 🪰 *Drosophila melanogaster* vs *Bactrocera oleae* — Comparative Summary

---

### 🧬 **Phylogeny**
- Both belong to **Order Diptera (Suborder Brachycera)**.  
- Diverged approximately **70 million years ago (MYA)**.  
- They are **more closely related to each other** than either is to **_Aedes aegypti_** (~150 MYA divergence).

---

### 🧫 **Ovary Maturation**
- In both species, ovaries are **immature at eclosion**.  
- **_D. melanogaster_** reaches full maturity in **2–3 days** (requires yeast protein).  
- **_B. oleae_** matures in **6–10 days** (requires external protein sources).

---

### 🍯 **Diet**
- Both need **sugars for energy** and **proteins for oogenesis**.  
- *Drosophila* uses **yeast** as its primary protein source.  
- *Bactrocera* relies on **microbial or natural protein sources** (e.g., bird droppings, baits).

---

### ⚥ **Sexual Dimorphism**
- **Females are larger** in both species, with increased abdominal volume for egg production.  
- *Drosophila* males have **sex combs**; *Bactrocera* females possess a **strong ovipositor** for fruit penetration.

---

### 🐛 **Development & Lifespan**
| Species | Egg → Adult | Adult Lifespan (@25°C) |
|----------|--------------|-------------------------|
| *Drosophila melanogaster* | ~10 days | 30–50 days |
| *Bactrocera oleae* | ~30–40 days | 1–2 months |

---

### 🧠 **Reproduction & Fecundity**
- Regulated by **juvenile hormone (JH)**, **insulin/TOR**, and **ecdysone** pathways.  
- **_D. melanogaster_**: early maturity, **high fecundity (~1000+ eggs/female)**.  
- **_B. oleae_**: delayed maturity, **moderate fecundity (~300–500 eggs/female)**.  
- In both, **oviposition onset depends on post-eclosion protein intake**.

---

### 🧩 **Summary**
- Both are evolutionarily close fruit flies sharing **homologous endocrine, developmental, and reproductive mechanisms**.  
- **_D. melanogaster_**: faster development, higher fecundity, saprophytic lifestyle.  
- **_B. oleae_**: slower development, host-specific (olive fruits), lower fecundity, field-adapted.
